# Strategic Progression Canon v1

## Core Doctrine Shift (Locked)
After the civilian-mother kill incident, the core team vows not to solve all major problems militarily.
They pivot to a long-war strategy of **out-teching and out-industrializing Consensus**.

## Geostrategic Constraint (Locked)
Consensus control is strongest from Mars inward.
The team’s viable growth route is Belt/out-system scaling: industry, fabrication, logistics, and coalition density.

## Conflict Pressure (Locked)
Despite strategic restraint, kinetic conflict remains unavoidable due to:
- Belt faction pressure and opportunistic conflict,
- Consensus spec-ops warforms/non-conscious puppets.

## Progression Feel (Locked)
Narrative progression should feel like order-of-magnitude capability growth (LitRPG-adjacent feel without explicit game framing).
Use occasional hard numbers, but prioritize felt momentum and consequence.

## Four Progression Tracks (Locked)
1. **Compute / Intelligence** — Nex capability, planning depth, inference quality.
2. **Industrial Throughput** — fabrication class, production rates, repair/refit tempo, supply resilience.
3. **Mobility / Space Control** — thrust-envelope leverage, route freedom, orbital initiative.
4. **Coalition / Legitimacy** — allied trust, recruitment, political room to maneuver.

## Book-Level Advancement Doctrine (Locked)
Per book, advance:
- 1–2 tracks significantly,
- 1–2 tracks partially,
so readers feel uninterrupted long-arc growth without flattening tension.

## Tone Guardrail
Brutality remains tactical; moral gravity remains human.
Even justified warform kills should carry second-order consequences (resource, escalation, political, psychological).
