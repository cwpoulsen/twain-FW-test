## Integration Module 1: Cross-Domain Story Utility

This module translates diplomacy failure, sanction-bypass industry, scarcity governance, and lane adjudication into scene-ready pressure chains for the ten-book arc. Book-level design uses mechanism -> decision -> cost cadence: negotiators choose between legitimacy and speed; industrial actors choose between survivability and throughput; governors choose between equality rhetoric and infrastructure triage; judges choose between sovereignty purity and civilian continuity.

For continuity safety, each module binds to fixed locks. Blackfly remains a post-EDF free-operator crew with constrained Class 2 leverage and uncertain location traceability under nuke masking history. Asterion League appears as a fragile but real Class 1-bearing coalition rather than a consolidated state. Consensus power is strong in institutions but weak at latency edges where command loops desynchronize and local improvisation dominates.

Scene deployment guidance: pair every negotiation chamber scene with a dock-floor consequence beat within one narrative cycle. Pair every sanction-bypass technical explanation with a household ration impact beat. Pair every adjudication ruling with convoy crew fatigue accounting, repair debt, and future political backlash. This keeps legal and economic exposition tied to character stakes instead of abstract policy monologue.

Campaign-scale outcome across modules is cumulative abrasion, not sudden collapse. Institutions bend, populations adapt, and legitimacy is renegotiated corridor by corridor. Victory conditions therefore measure durability of humane process under scarcity, not clean battlefield annihilation.

## Integration Module 2: Failure-to-Legitimacy Conversion Arcs

Use this module when a coalition actor must turn a diplomatic or legal failure into a survivable legitimacy strategy without violating canon locks. The sequence begins with public admission of bounded control, then publication of hard constraints (fuel, berth capacity, legal service delay), then an immediately observable fairness gesture such as rotating district power cuts instead of hidden preferential allocation.

Narratively, this gives protagonists room to win trust by process rather than by impossible supply miracles. It also protects continuity against sudden competence inflation. Under Reality Gate conditions, institutions improve by reducing hidden variance and by shortening decision loops they actually control.

At scene level, pair announcements with resistant audiences: dock unions, casualty families, and tribunal clerks. Let characters debate whether transparency invites exploitation; then show how controlled disclosure reduces FVB rumor leverage and lowers violence probability in subsequent convoy queues.

## Integration Module 3: Adjudication-to-Operations Feedback

This module tracks how lane-court precedents alter military and industrial behavior over time. If tribunals repeatedly penalize indiscriminate seizure, patrol commanders shift toward targeted escrow and evidence discipline. If courts reward documentation quality, sanction-bypass networks invest in forged provenance and the enforcement game moves to metadata integrity rather than brute interdiction.

The important series function is recursive consequence. No ruling is an endpoint: each judgment changes optimization strategies for smugglers, governors, escorts, and insurers. Build book transitions around that adaptation loop so legal drama and tactical action remain inseparable.

Continuity guard: keep warforms as instruments, not legal persons; keep Blackfly as uncertainly located independent operators; keep Asterion as fragile coalition with nontrivial internal disagreement.

## Integration Module 4: Convoy Scarcity Governance Episode Templates

Template A (Municipal Fracture): A convoy arrives twenty hours late with mixed cargo that cannot satisfy all districts. Council leaders must choose whether to prioritize systems stability (oxygen scrubber membranes, coolant additives) or household calorie distribution. The dramatic engine is not villainy but triage arithmetic. Write the scene with visible queue boards, union observers, and legal clerks annotating which emergency code is invoked. Then cut to domestic consequences in under-supplied rings.

Template B (Sanction Shock): Enforcement teams freeze a suspected bypass network account tree and accidentally capture legitimate med logistics. Councils face a legitimacy cliff: defend enforcement and accept deaths from delay, or override seizure and risk appearing captured by smugglers. Resolve with provisional escrow orders, not perfect exoneration. Show the paperwork burden and fatigue on operators who must execute nuanced policy while sleep-deprived.

Template C (Lane Court Compression): A judge receives stale telemetry and contradictory beacon logs before issuing temporary possession orders. The key dramatic move is epistemic humility under time pressure. The ruling should preserve life-support continuity while admitting uncertainty and setting a mandatory evidence refresh window. Later chapters can revisit liability once better data arrives.

Template D (Diplomacy Re-entry): After a failed summit, negotiators return with narrower scope and explicit civilian-loss counters tied to each delay interval. Stakes become measurable: every six-hour stall projects additional ration cuts, med denial, or maintenance deferral. Characters who once grandstand now bargain in constrained increments because institutions have learned the cost of performative maximalism.

These templates are continuity-safe because they foreground locked constraints: no instant command omniscience, no inexhaustible industrial capacity, no seamless social compliance, and no single decree that harmonizes fractured lane politics. They also preserve ideological complexity. Steward actors can be strategically rational and morally corrosive at once; Asterion actors can be principled yet factionally brittle; civilian councils can be compassionate while reproducing hierarchy through labor-weighted access schemes.

For long-arc placement, deploy Template A/B heavily in Books 2-4 to establish scarcity governance grammar; use Template C in Books 3-7 to build jurisprudential continuity; use Template D recurrently through Books 5-10 as reconquest politics shifts from seizure to administrability. This creates a coherent evolution from survival improvisation toward contested institutional settlement without violating the canon rule that every gain carries downstream cost.
