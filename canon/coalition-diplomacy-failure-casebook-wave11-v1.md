## Case 1: Helion Grain Accords Collapse

### Trigger and Negotiation Frame
Asterion municipal delegates demanded transparent Class 2 spare-part audits before signing convoy grain guarantees. Steward intermediaries treated disclosure as intelligence leakage and substituted redacted payload attestations.

### Failure Mechanics
Belt ratifiers interpreted redactions as proof that relief shipments were bundled with covert surveillance hardware. Three compact councils suspended corridor cooperation, and neutral tug unions refused bonded escorts.

### Systemic Cost
Food reserves in two spin habitats dropped below ninety-six hours, forcing emergency ration codes and riot-prevention curfews. Blackfly cells lost a planned intake window because docking berths were reallocated to medical freight.

### Continuity-Safe Lessons
Asterion brokers later rebuilt talks using split-ledger verification: humanitarian cargo manifests were verified by independent dock mathematicians while security-sensitive metadata stayed escrowed. The model restored minimal trust but increased latency and berth turnaround overhead.

### Trigger and Negotiation Frame

## Case 7: Diplomatic Failure Pattern 7

In this pattern, coalition diplomats attempt to solve an immediate convoy, fuel, or prisoner emergency by fusing unrelated sovereignty demands into the relief instrument. The mechanism repeatedly fails because latency, distrust, and fragmented command chains make conditional aid look like strategic blackmail. Blackfly intelligence notes show that even pragmatic municipal actors become maximalist when families read a clause as irreversible loss of local agency.

Operationally, the failure begins with document asymmetry. One side circulates machine-dense annexes only to military lawyers; the other side circulates oral summaries to councils under time pressure. By the time full text arrives, convoys are already delayed and dock queues are saturated. FVB brokers then sell forged 'clarifications' to desperate captains, further degrading shared facts. Because Reality Gate constraints prohibit instant command correction, each correction arrives too late to prevent local overreaction.

The immediate outputs are predictable: convoy holds, emergency requisition orders, ration compression, and urban rumor cascades. The deeper continuity consequence is institutional fatigue. Each failed summit increases procedural overhead in the next one: more observers, more escrow, more conditional signatures, and narrower authority mandates for delegates. Diplomacy therefore becomes slower precisely when scarcity demands speed.

Continuity-safe doctrine recommends three guardrails. First, keep life-support cargo agreements textually separate from status or jurisdiction settlements. Second, require precomputed latency calendars so no side can exploit delayed clarifications as tactical ambiguity. Third, attach consequence ledgers that force negotiators to publish expected civilian losses if talks stall beyond specific hour marks.

## Case 16: Relay-Custody Recognition Deadlock

### Trigger and Negotiation Frame
Asterion mediators proposed that emergency relay stations be treated as neutral humanitarian infrastructure even when physically hosted in Compact jurisdictions sympathetic to Blackfly couriers. Steward delegates accepted neutrality in principle but required remote firmware escrow keys that would permit later forensic extraction by Consensus investigators.

### Failure Mechanics
Compact councils interpreted escrow as retroactive intelligence annexation and refused key deposit. Without mutual recognition, relay stations downshifted to local traffic only, severing long-haul legal notice flows for appeals, cargo stays, and casualty notifications. Judges in three lane courts began issuing non-enforceable placeholder rulings because party service could not be confirmed inside statutory windows.

### Systemic Cost
Administrative darkness multiplied rumor governance. Families with detained relatives received contradictory reports and paid predatory brokers for unverifiable updates. Convoy insurers priced uncertainty surcharges into all routes crossing the dark segment, diverting cargo from already fragile civilian habitats. The political effect was corrosive: each bloc blamed the other for broken due process while simultaneously benefiting from plausible deniability.

### Continuity-Safe Lessons
The eventual bridge model separated relay neutrality from investigative access. Stations now maintain one-way legal notification channels with cryptographic attestations while any forensic pull requires a separate tribunal warrant signed by mixed-jurisdiction judges. This design is slower than unilateral escrow but prevents emergency communications infrastructure from becoming a covert capture mechanism.

## Case 17: Armistice Language Drift Across Latency Bands

### Trigger and Negotiation Frame
An armistice text approved near Earth used legal terms whose Belt practice meanings had shifted over years of scarcity governance. By the time authenticated copies reached outer lanes, councils had already implemented local interpretations that contradicted steward military intent.

### Failure Mechanics
Because one-way delay exceeded political reaction time, each side treated the other's implementation as intentional deception. Tactical patrols detained survey craft under one reading while municipal courts ordered release under another. The legal conflict escalated into kinetic warning burns that consumed propellant needed for medical convoy shepherding.

### Systemic Cost
Civilian administrators absorbed cascading burden: court dockets tripled, depot inventories misaligned, and fatigue errors increased among overworked signal clerks reconciling version trees. The episode demonstrated that text alone cannot carry legitimacy across lag-distorted governance space.

### Continuity-Safe Lessons
Armistice packets now include executable interpretation tables, glossary hashes, and scenario examples validated by both blocs before release. The governance insight is procedural humility: in a latency-bound system, semantic precision is humanitarian infrastructure, not legal ornament.

## Meta-Pattern Note: Why Failure Persists Despite Experienced Negotiators

Coalition diplomacy fails repeatedly not because parties lack technical competence, but because institutional incentives reward positional signaling over corridor survivability during early bargaining rounds. Delegates are evaluated by internal hardliners watching delayed transcripts, not by immediate civilian outcomes in distant habitats. Under latency, that audience effect worsens: by the time practical concessions prove life-saving, political patrons may already have condemned them as weakness.

Continuity-safe storytelling should therefore depict negotiators as trapped actors optimizing under misaligned accountability clocks. Successful agreements emerge only when process redesign changes who bears visible blame for delay. Mechanisms include civilian-loss counters, mandatory publication of hold-duration consequences, and mixed verification teams that reduce opportunities for curated ambiguity. These tools do not eliminate conflict; they alter payoff geometry so procedural cooperation becomes politically survivable.
