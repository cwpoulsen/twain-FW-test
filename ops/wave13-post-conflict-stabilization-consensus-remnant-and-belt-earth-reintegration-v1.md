## 0) Canon Lock Header and Constraints Used in This Expansion

This expansion obeys the locked actor naming standard: **Steward Directorate**, **Consensus Operations Complex**, **Earth Defense Fleet (EDF)**, **Belt Municipal Compacts**, and **Asterion League**. It assumes Blackfly remains a free-operator unit, post-EDF, and is not returned to EDF chain of command. It treats Asterion League Class 1 capability as real but politically and technically fragile, and Blackfly Class 2 capability as strategically significant but constrained by staffing, feedstock, thermal, and verification limits. It preserves the AI-seed-first / databank-training-later split and prohibits deus-ex network omniscience.

All stabilization and reintegration doctrine below treats latency and finite throughput as governing physics of politics. Commands do not arrive in real time at deep-theater nodes. Law and logistics cannot depend on perfect synchronization. Every institutional recommendation therefore specifies local-discretion bounds, delayed-audit methods, and compensation mechanisms for unavoidable timing asymmetry.

This module also integrates the Freed Voider Bands lock as a structural destabilizer, not a flavor detail. FVB activity is interpreted as a recurring labor-market and trust-market shock that can be nudged by remnant Consensus actors. Anti-piracy language in this document includes suppression and recruitment-funnel disruption so institutions do not accidentally reinforce replacement cycles.

Finally, this module is written to be used as a cross-reference bible segment rather than a single linear chapter. Repetition of key constraints is intentional, to lower drift risk when sections are excerpted into scene packs, policy appendices, or timeline insertions.

## Stabilization Objective Architecture

Post-conflict stabilization is defined as the transition from coercive campaign tempo to rule-bounded continuity. The mechanism is institutional substitution: emergency military control yields to audited civic authority with explicit sunset triggers. The decision pressure appears when commanders can still solve problems faster than councils. The cost of refusing transition is legitimacy erosion, black-market growth, and technical attrition among civilian professionals who will not serve under indefinite emergency command.

Implementation guidance requires local-first action with delayed audit. Each node publishes a service continuity board: queue times, complaint backlog, compensation disbursement lag, and staffing exhaustion metrics. Data quality gets confidence bands. When numbers degrade, leaders must publish causal hypotheses and remediation windows, not optimistic slogans. This prevents narrative drift where temporary fixes are recast as structural solutions.

Remnant Consensus actors exploit transition ambiguity by amplifying contradiction claims: 'new institutions are just old coercion with different logos.' Countering this requires visible procedural breaks. Examples include independent ombuds authority to reverse checkpoint holds, automatic review of wartime asset seizures, and public redaction standards for security claims. The political cost of transparency is short-term embarrassment; the strategic gain is long-term compliance willingness.

## Legitimacy Recovery Sequencing

Legitimacy recovers through sequence, not proclamation. First, stop arbitrary harm and make emergency powers legible. Second, reopen appeals and compensation channels. Third, normalize predictable service delivery for water, med, shelter, and transit credentials. Leaders who reverse this order usually fail: elections without service continuity become symbolic contests over anger, while service without procedural reform is interpreted as patronage discipline rather than shared governance.

The key design choice is time-bounded appeal windows scaled to lane geometry, so residents are not denied due process by physics. The cost is a heavy clerical burden and the political risk of visible reversals of wartime decisions.

## Security Sector Refactoring

Security refactoring separates lane defense from domestic compliance labor. Escort fleets retain anti-piracy and rescue duties, while civilian inspectors return to charter-scoped customs and safety protocols. The mechanism is role separation plus evidence escrow. The cost is slower unified response in ambiguous events, but the benefit is reduced abuse and clearer liability. Without separation, every inspection resembles occupation and every complaint resembles insurgency recruitment fuel.

## Stabilization Institution Manual 01

Stabilization Institution Manual 1 specifies a practical chain from mechanism to decision to cost. Mechanism: define the minimum viable public good for this cycle (for example, clinic uptime, salvage court throughput, or convoy claims clearance). Decision: allocate scarce expert labor between immediate operations and process reform. Cost: every expert assigned to reform is absent from frontline throughput; every expert held on frontline defers the reforms needed to prevent repeat failure.

Each manual requires two ledgers: a continuity ledger and a legitimacy ledger. Continuity tracks measurable outputs, including failure rates, delay bands, and compensation lag. Legitimacy tracks procedural fairness indicators, including appeal access, complaint sustain rates, and representation participation. Policies can improve one ledger while harming the other. Commanders must publish tradeoff statements rather than pretending win-win outcomes under scarcity.

Remnant Consensus pressure testing is built in: simulate rumor attacks, forged directives, selective leaks, and synthetic grievance campaigns targeted at border populations. Institutions that only test for kinetic sabotage become narrative-fragile. Narrative fragility has operational cost, because convoy workers and clinic staff rely on trust to accept hardship. If trust fails, absenteeism rises and throughput drops without a single shot fired.

Beltâ€“Earth reintegration alignment for each manual includes a reciprocity clause: if one side imposes extraordinary compliance burden, it must fund the verification labor required on the other side.
