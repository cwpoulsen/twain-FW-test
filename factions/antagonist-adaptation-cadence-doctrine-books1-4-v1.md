## Escalation Branches (Antagonist Decision Tree)

1. **Containment Branch** (preferred in Books 1–2)
   - Goal: localize protagonist success.
   - Methods: censorship, selective arrests, quiet financial sanctions.
   - Exit criteria: no copycat operations for 2 cycles.

2. **Deterrence Branch** (transition in Book 2)
   - Goal: make operations too costly.
   - Methods: visible punitive examples, asset seizure, family-pressure operations.
   - Risk: creates martyrs and fuels protagonist recruitment.

3. **Fragmentation Branch** (dominant in Book 3)
   - Goal: break trust inside protagonist network.
   - Methods: forged communications, controlled leaks, induced ideological splits.
   - Risk: high information operations budget; difficult to reverse once exposed.

4. **Totalization Branch** (Book 4 crisis mode)
   - Goal: convert conflict into existential framing.
   - Methods: emergency decrees, private militias, broad legal exceptionalism.
   - Risk: cross-bloc legitimacy collapse and defections to SAN.

## Doctrine Rules of Thumb

- Antagonists learn fastest from **public defeats**, not private losses.
- Each successful protagonist innovation buys roughly **one operation cycle** before hard counters emerge.
- Overreaction creates exploitable seams; underreaction creates replication risk.
- SAN becomes stronger when other blocs fail to share lessons quickly.
